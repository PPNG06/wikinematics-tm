Until here, we only saw cases where the velocity was constant. Basically, it means that we only had to care about displacement and time, since velocity didn't change. However, in many everyday situations, an object's velocity isn't fixed. Cars accelerate, airplanes take off, and life isn't fixed at a constant speed, right ?

What is acceleration and how do we define it in kinematics ?


Well, the definition of acceleration is quite intuitive: it is the rate of change of the velocity of an object with respect to time..

Two words are important here: rate of change, and with respect to time


Indeed, a very simple explanation is that if an object's acceleration is 4 m/s2 (we will talk about units later), it means that every second its velocity will increase by 4 m/s. Similarly, if its acceleration (rather deceleration) is -4 m/s2, it means that every second its velocity will decrease by 4 m/s

Units:

As you may have seen, acceleration is defined in meters per second squared (m/s2). This unit may not seem as intuitive as previous ones (meters, seconds, meters per second, etc..), but in reality, it is purely logical.


Recall that acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with respect to time:


As said before, if an object's acceleration is 4 m/s2, it means that it will gain 4 m/s every second.

Therefore, it will gain 8 m/s after 2 seconds, 12 m/s after 3 seconds, etc.. right ?


This means that acceleration multiplied by time has to give back a unit of velocity (m/s), and thus "something" multiplied by seconds has to give back meters per seconds:

This all comes back into one sentence: "Acceleration multiplied by time represents an increase (or decrease if acceleration is negative) in velocity"

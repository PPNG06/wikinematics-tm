What is Uniformly Accelerated Circular Motion (UACM)?


As usual, everything is in the name. There are two important things, here:


  • "Circular" tells us that we will be looking at angles, radians, angular velocities, etc... (recall Speed: Uniform Circular Motion)
  • "Uniformly Accelerated" tells us that there will be some sort of acceleration, and angular velocity won't remain constant today!

For a more formal definition, UACM is a movement of a body follwing a circular path, where the rate of rotation changes linearly over time.



Angular Acceleration:

Remember how we defined angular velocity in Speed: Uniform Circular Motion ? Well, for UACM, we will have to similarly define an angular acceleration.



Just like linear acceleration refers to the rate of change of velocity over time, angular acceleration refers to the rate of change of angular velocity over time

Put simply, it answers the question "how much more radians per second do we have, per second ?"

Uniformly Accelerated Rectilinear Motion: what is it?


In order for a motion to be UARM, it has to satisfy two properties:

  • Acceleration has to be constant/uniform (velocity increases in a linear way)
  • The trajectory has to remain linear (in 2D)

Some examples of UARM include: a car accelerating on a straight road, an airplane during take-off, or even just a ball going down a slope!

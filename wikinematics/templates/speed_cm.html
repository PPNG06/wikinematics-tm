What is circular motion ?


While rectilinear motion may seem quite intuitive, circular motion can appear as being a bit more complicated... Be safe, it is not.

I mean, everything is in the name: circular motion is the movement of an object that rotates along a circular path


The rotation has to follow two rules:

  • Its trajectory has to be perfectly round (no ellipses)
  • It has to be fixed around an axis

Just remember one thing: here, we will only discuss uniform circular motion (which means that objects will always be rotating at constant speed). If you want to know what happens when objects accelerate, feel free to read about UACM



And the formulas?

Well, before figuring out the formulas, maybe we should define what we are looking for..? Remember, the motion is fixed around an axis, which means that the distance from the starting point never changes right ? It will always be equal to the radius.


And that's where the angles come into play.

(Recall Intro: angles)

While we will not measure distance (simply because it doesn't change), we can measure the displacement by measuring the angle between the starting and ending point!

Period and frequency:

There is one more thing that we should talk about when describing circular motion

  • The period (often noted ) describes the time in seconds required for the object to make a full circle (=travel 360 degrees or 2πrad)

Centripetal Acceleration

Lastly, let me introduce a little theoretical notion here:


For an object to be in a circular movement, by nature, even if the object's rotating velocity is constant, it has to be accelerating towards the center of its trajectory (centripetal). This is because if it didn't, the object would simply drift out of the trajectory and "nothing would retain it from escaping the circle".

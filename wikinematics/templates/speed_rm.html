What is Rectilinear motion ?


Well, it is that simple: Rectilinear motion is movement in 1D. Basically, it means that movements (e.g: left or right) can be described by a simple straight line.


In this section, we will discuss uniform rectilinear motion (without acceleration, the simplest case). If you want to know what happens when objects accelerate, feel free to read about UARM



Little comment about frames of reference:

For more details, please go to Kinematics: an Introduction


The first thing we want to do when describing a rectilinear motion is define the positive/negative direction.

Why ? Well, let's consider the following case:

If two cars are moving in the same direction, at same speed, it seems intuitive that the distance between them will never increase, right ?

Now what if they move in opposite direction ? The distance between the two cars will continuously increase as the two cars are moving away!


This means that in directions matter when talking about motion!

And to deal with the matter, we just arbitrarly define one direction as the positive direction, and the other one as the negative direction.

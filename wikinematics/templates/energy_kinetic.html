Kinetic energy

It seems intuitive that any object moving possess some sort of energy right ? Well, kinetic energy is the energy relative to an object's speed. Any object in motion is using kinetic energy: a thrown ball, a person running, or even a charged particle in an electric field.



Kinetic energy: the formula


Let's first think intuitively. On what should an object's kinetic energy depend ? The first main obvious answer is speed, of course (notice that we talk about speed and not velocity here, because energy is always positive)

But there is something else that kinetic energy depends on. For example, it wouldn't take the same energy for a tennis ball or a car to move at the same speed, right ? In other words, it would require more energy for a car to move than for a tennis ball at same speed. This means that the object's mass has to somehow play a role in the object's kinetic energy.

The main takeaways for this formula are that

  • For a given object, the only way for its kinetic energy to increase/decrease is by increasing/decreasing its speed
